## Overview

Corporate governance and compliance in Saudi Arabia is undergoing rapid modernization, driven by **Vision 2030** and alignment with international best practices. This guide covers the four pillars every enterprise must address:

- **CMA Regulations** — Capital market oversight for listed entities
- **ZATCA Compliance** — Tax, Zakat, VAT, and customs obligations
- **ESG Reporting** — Sustainability disclosure frameworks
- **Board Governance** — Structure, independence, and committee requirements

> **Note:** Saudi Arabia's governance ecosystem is mandatory for listed companies under CMA regulations and advisory for unlisted entities under Ministry of Commerce guidelines.

## 1. CMA Regulations (Capital Market Authority)

### Scope
CMA Regulations apply to:
- Listed Joint Stock Companies (JSCs) on **Tadawul** (Main Market) — **binding**
- Companies on **Nomu** (Parallel Market) — partially binding
- Securities firms, investment funds, and financial intermediaries

### Core Compliance Obligations

| Obligation | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Financial Reporting** | Audited quarterly and annual financial statements |
| **Continuous Disclosure** | Material events reported immediately to Tadawul |
| **AML/CFT** | Anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing compliance |
| **Prospectus Requirements** | Full prospectus for securities offerings |
| **Capital Adequacy** | Minimum capital requirements for market intermediaries |
| **Insider Trading** | Strict prohibition with criminal referral powers |

### CMA Enforcement Powers
- Impose financial penalties
- Refer cases for criminal prosecution
- Bar individuals from securities business
- Suspend or cancel licenses

### CMA Strategic Roadmap (2024–2026)
The CMA launched the **"Development of the Corporate Governance Regulations"** initiative under its 2024–2026 strategic plan, scheduled for completion by 2026. Key focus areas:
- Strengthening board independence requirements
- Enhancing minority shareholder protections
- Expanding sustainability disclosure linkage to capital-raising

### Recent Updates
- **January 2025:** CMA approved controls allowing foreign investment in listed companies owning real estate in Makkah and Madinah
- **May 2025:** New sustainable finance regulations linking ESG disclosure to capital-raising took effect
- **2026:** Full CGR development initiative completion expected

## 2. ZATCA Compliance (Tax, Zakat & Customs)

### Saudi Arabia's Dual-Track Tax System

Saudi Arabia operates a unique fiscal system based on ownership structure:

```
Saudi / GCC Ownership  →  ZAKAT (2.5% of Zakat base)
Foreign Ownership      →  Corporate Income Tax (CIT) at 20%
Mixed Ownership        →  Split proportionally by ownership percentage
```

**Example:** A company that is 60% Saudi-owned and 40% foreign-owned pays:
- Zakat on 60% of the Zakat base
- CIT at 20% on 40% of adjusted net profits

---

### Zakat

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Rate** | 2.5% of the Zakat base |
| **Base** | Net equity adjusted for provisions, retained earnings, long-term financing |
| **Applicability** | Saudi and GCC-national owned entities |
| **Filing Deadline** | Within 120 days of fiscal year-end |

---

### Corporate Income Tax (CIT)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Rate** | 20% of net adjusted profits |
| **Applicability** | Foreign-owned entities, permanent establishments |
| **Withholding Tax (WHT)** | Applies to royalties, technical fees, dividends paid to non-residents |
| **Transfer Pricing** | Annual documentation required |
| **Filing Deadline** | Within 120 days of fiscal year-end |

---

### VAT

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Standard Rate** | 15% |
| **Mandatory Registration** | Annual taxable supplies > **SAR 375,000** |
| **Voluntary Registration** | Annual supplies between SAR 187,500 – 375,000 |
| **Filing Frequency** | Monthly (supplies > SAR 40M/year) or Quarterly |
| **April 2025 Update** | ZATCA approved amendments to VAT Implementing Regulations |
| **VAT Grouping** | Effective October 15, 2025 |
| **E-Marketplace (Deemed Supplier)** | Effective January 1, 2026 |

---

### E-Invoicing (FATOORA) — Phase 2

FATOORA Phase 2 is the **integration phase**, requiring taxpayers to connect their invoicing systems directly to ZATCA's platform.

**Rollout approach:**
- Implemented in **waves** by revenue threshold
- ZATCA provides **6 months notice** before each group's compliance date
- Phase 2 requirements: specific e-invoice format, additional fields, QR code, real-time/near-real-time reporting

> **Wave 14 (February 2025):** Taxpayers with VAT-liable revenues exceeding SAR 5 million in 2022 or 2023.

---

### ZATCA Compliance Calendar

| Obligation | Deadline |
|-----------|---------|
| Annual CIT / Zakat Return | 120 days after fiscal year-end |
| Audited Financial Statements | With annual return |
| Transfer Pricing Documentation | Annual — updated each year |
| VAT Return (monthly filers) | By 20th of following month |
| VAT Return (quarterly filers) | By 20th of month following quarter |
| WHT Remittance | 10th of following month |
| FATOORA compliance | Per wave notification |

---

### Common Compliance Pitfalls

- ❌ Delayed VAT registration → retroactive liabilities + penalties
- ❌ Incorrect WHT rates or late remittance
- ❌ No transfer pricing documentation for related-party transactions
- ❌ Failure to integrate with FATOORA platform on time
- ❌ Misclassifying mixed-ownership tax base between Zakat and CIT

## 3. ESG & Sustainability Reporting

### Current Status (2025–2026)

> ESG reporting in Saudi Arabia remains **largely guidance-based** rather than fully mandatory — but the regulatory direction is clearly toward mandatory standards aligned with IFRS S1 & S2.

### Applicable Frameworks

| Framework | Description | Status in KSA |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Saudi Exchange ESG Guidelines** | Tadawul's reporting framework (2021) | Voluntary — widely adopted |
| **GRI Standards** | Global Reporting Initiative | Widely used |
| **TCFD** | Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures | Recommended |
| **SASB** | Sustainability Accounting Standards Board | Sector-specific use |
| **IFRS S1** | General sustainability-related financial disclosures | Forthcoming mandatory |
| **IFRS S2** | Climate-related disclosures (governance, strategy, risk, metrics) | Forthcoming mandatory |

---

### IFRS S1 & S2 — What to Expect

**IFRS S1** requires companies to disclose:
- Relevant ESG topics and their financial impact
- Sustainability-related risks and opportunities
- How ESG factors affect enterprise value

**IFRS S2** requires disclosure of:
- Board/management governance over climate risks
- Climate strategy and scenario analysis
- Risk management processes for climate
- Climate-related metrics and targets (including Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions)

---

### ESG Disclosure Trends

| Year | Top 100 Issuers Disclosing | Total Companies |
|------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 2023 | 58% | ~80 companies |
| 2024 | 65% | 94 companies |
| 2025+ | Accelerating | Mandatory regime approaching |

**Leading disclosers:** Saudi Aramco, SABIC, **STC**, Saudi Electricity Company

---

### Board's Role in ESG

Boards must:
- Oversee sustainability strategy and set targets
- Embed ESG into decision-making processes
- Establish a **dedicated ESG Committee** or assign ESG responsibilities to an existing committee
- Ensure alignment with IFRS S1/S2 when adopted
- Monitor and approve annual sustainability reports

---

### ESG Business Case

- ESG-compliant businesses in Saudi Arabia reported a **20% increase in FDI** in 2024
- CMA is linking sustainability disclosure to **capital-raising eligibility**
- A dedicated **ESG Index on Tadawul** spotlights best-practice companies
- Global investors increasingly require ESG alignment before committing capital

---

### Action Steps for Organizations

1. Download and map to Saudi Exchange ESG Disclosure Guidelines
2. Conduct a **materiality assessment** to identify priority ESG topics
3. Align data collection to GRI / TCFD / ISSB frameworks
4. Begin **voluntary reporting** to build readiness for mandatory regime
5. Establish board-level ESG oversight structure

## 4. Board Governance Requirements

### Board Structure

Saudi Arabia operates a **single-tier board structure** where executive and non-executive directors sit together on the same board.

| Requirement | Rule |
|-------------|------|
| **Independent Directors** | At least 2, or one-third of the board — whichever is greater |
| **Non-Executive Majority** | Majority of board must be non-executive |
| **Chair/CEO Separation** | **Mandatory** — chair and CEO must be different persons |
| **Board Training** | Mandatory for board and executive members (Jan 2024 amendment) |
| **Cumulative Voting** | Saudi Arabia is the only country mandating it for board elections |

---

### Director Independence Criteria

A director is **NOT independent** if they:

- Have material business relationships with the company or its affiliates
- Hold **5% or more** of company shares (directly or indirectly)
- Have **family relationships** with executive management or major shareholders
- Were **employed by the company** within the past **5 years**
- Served as **external auditor** for the company within the past **2 years**
- Serve as executive director of another company where the company's executives also serve as directors

---

### Mandatory Board Committees

#### 🔍 Audit Committee
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Size** | 3–5 members |
| **Composition** | All members must be non-executive; at least one independent director |
| **Financial Expertise** | At least one member specialized in finance/accounting |
| **Key Roles** | Financial reporting oversight, external/internal auditor appointment, risk review |
| **Jan 2024 Update** | Must issue recommendations on internal auditor appointment; periodic meetings with auditors required |

#### 🏅 Nominations & Remuneration Committee
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Composition** | Majority independent non-executives |
| **Key Roles** | Board nominations, director remuneration policy, performance linkage |
| **2022 Law Change** | Fixed pay cap removed; replaced with principle-based "fair, incentivizing, and performance-commensurate" standard |

#### ⚠️ Risk Management Committee
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Key Roles** | Risk appetite framework, risk oversight, escalation to full board |
| **Trend** | Increasingly covering cyber risk, regulatory risk, and ESG risk |

---

### Internal Audit Requirements (Mandatory since January 2024)

Under **Articles 73–75 of the CGR** (now mandatory):

- Establish a **formal Internal Audit Unit**
- Adopt an **annual Internal Audit Plan**
- Prepare and submit an **Internal Audit Report**
- Audit Committee must recommend appointment/dismissal of internal auditor

---

### Board Diversity

Research findings on Saudi-listed companies (2021–2023):

| Diversity Factor | Impact on ESG Disclosure |
|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Female representation | ✅ Positive correlation |
| Professional background & skills | ✅ Positive correlation |
| Board compensation alignment | ✅ Positive correlation |
| Board size alone | ❌ No significant impact |
| Formal independence alone | ❌ No significant impact |

> **Implication:** Substance matters more than structure. Boards should prioritize **cognitive diversity** — different professional backgrounds, functional expertise, and thinking styles — not just box-ticking on independence.
